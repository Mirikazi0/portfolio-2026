The Company

Symph is a software development studio based in Cebu. During my internship, I was embedded within their design team and assigned as the primary designer for Lesson Planner PH — an EdTech product helping Filipino teachers build structured lesson plans faster.

I was tasked with designing the product's entire landing experience from scratch — no prior design work to reference, no established visual language to follow.

The Problem

The product existed but had no landing page — meaning it had no way to communicate its value to teachers or build credibility with institutions considering adoption.

The challenge: the landing page needed to speak to two audiences with very different needs at the same time — individual teachers who needed to feel it saves real time, and institutions that needed to trust it before recommending it.

What I did

I started with structure, not visuals. Before touching Figma, I mapped the content hierarchy — problem → solution → proof → contact — to ensure the page earned trust in the right sequence.

After validating the structure through lo-fi and mid-fidelity wireframes, I moved into high-fidelity design — building a visual language that felt credible and approachable for both teacher and institutional audiences.

Final Output

The high-fidelity landing page led with a clear outcome statement for teachers, then built the case section by section — use cases, feature value, social proof, and a dedicated B2B contact entry point for institutions.

Impact & Takeaways

The design gave Lesson Planner PH its first credible public presence and became the foundation for early B2B and institutional conversations. Framing outcomes over features allowed the page to serve both audiences without compromising either.

💡 Structure before screens. Mapping the content narrative first made the visual design process significantly faster and more intentional.

🎯 Landing pages serve multiple stakeholders. In EdTech, individual teacher appeal and institutional credibility have to coexist — and that tension shapes every design decision.

🔄 Working from zero builds range. Designing with no prior reference forced me to develop strong first-principles thinking around hierarchy and visual trust.
